Abstract

Pre-training video transformers remains computationally intensive, constrained by dense frame requirements and limited scalability across temporal scales and spatial resolutions. In this work, we present LLaVA-ViT, a cluster discrimination vision transformer for efficient and scalable video representation learning.

A core design of LLaVA-ViT is the C続-Input formulations with a 3D rotary positional encoding. Specifically, we introduce: (a) Dense video-codec decomposition inspired by HEVC to mask out ~90% redundant pixels while preserving temporal fidelity; (b) Chunk-wise sampling for adaptive frame-rate scalability; and (c) Cross-frame tiling for high-resolution spatial scalability.

Furthermore, our cluster discrimination contrastive objective leverages semantic clustering to jointly model object-level and motion-level semantics. Extensive experiments demonstrate that LLaVA-ViT achieves state-of-the-art performance under probing and fine-tuning across both image and video tasks.

The C続-Input Formulation

Three core strategies to unify dense input efficiency, temporal flexibility, and spatial scalability.

Dense Video-Codec

Inspired by HEVC. We decompose dense inputs into I-frames (complete context) and P-frames (residuals). Using motion vectors, we mask out ~90% of redundant pixels, focusing computation only on dynamic regions.

Chunk-wise Sampling

Dynamically selects frames from fixed temporal chunks. This ensures the model is robust to varying temporal densities and enables scalable frame-rate handling during inference.

Cross-frame Tiling

Vertically concatenates consecutive frames into a single high-resolution spatial grid. This expands spatial coverage, allowing the model to learn high-resolution details effectively.

Spatio-Temporal Efficiency

Visualization of the C続-Input strategy. (a) Decomposing dense videos, (b) Random sampling, and (c) Concatenating frames.

Cluster Discrimination

We employ a single-step offline clustering to capture both object-level semantics (images) and motion-level semantics (videos), aligning representations within semantic clusters while pushing dissimilar ones apart.
